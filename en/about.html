About Me

Mechanical Engineer focused on computational modeling, physics-based analysis, and the use of simulation to better understand real engineering systems.

Background

I am Thomas Allendes, a Mechanical Engineer with a Master’s in Computational Mechanics. My work focuses on computational engineering, with emphasis on CFD, FEM, heat transfer, and multiphysics modeling applied to real engineering problems.

I am particularly interested in using simulation not as an end in itself, but as a tool to better understand complex systems, reduce uncertainty, and support sound engineering decisions.

My Approach

Physics First

I focus on identifying the governing physics of a problem before deciding on the appropriate modeling strategy.

Appropriate Complexity

My goal is not to make models unnecessarily complex, but to use the right level of fidelity for the engineering question at hand.

Engineering Insight

I aim to translate numerical results into conclusions that are technically sound, physically consistent, and useful in practice.

Technical Focus

Computational Fluid Dynamics

Flow behavior, turbulence, mixing, transport phenomena, and thermal-fluid systems studied through physics-based numerical modeling.

Finite Element Modeling

Thermal and structural analysis of engineering systems, with attention to formulation, material behavior, and realistic boundary conditions.

Heat Transfer and Multiphysics

Conduction, convection, phase change, and coupled phenomena analyzed to better understand the behavior of complex systems.

Experience

My background includes both industrial engineering projects and research-oriented numerical work. This combination has allowed me to approach problems with technical depth while keeping a strong focus on practical relevance.

Across these experiences, I have worked on fluid flow, thermal systems, phase change modeling, and engineering analysis in contexts where physical consistency and decision support matter.
