Overview

This page documents a FreeFEM-based model intended to study lithium-ion battery behavior from a finite element perspective, with emphasis on thermal response, transport mechanisms, and numerical implementation.

The purpose is not only to present simulation outputs, but also to expose the structure of the model: assumptions, domain decomposition, governing equations, numerical strategy, and interpretation of results.

Engineering Context

Lithium-ion batteries are governed by coupled physical processes involving charge transport, heat generation, diffusion, and material-dependent behavior. From an engineering perspective, modeling these systems is useful for understanding performance, temperature rise, safety margins, and the effect of operating conditions.

Depending on the scope of the case, the analysis may focus on thermal behavior alone, or on a more coupled description involving electrochemical and transport effects.

Problem Definition

The objective of the model is to represent one or more relevant aspects of lithium-ion battery behavior under transient operating conditions. Typical quantities of interest may include:

  • Temperature evolution in time
  • Internal heat generation
  • Potential or concentration fields
  • Effect of material or geometric parameters
  • Response under charge or discharge conditions

This template can later be adapted depending on whether the case is mainly thermal, electrochemical, or multiphysics.

Geometry and Domains

The computational model may include different battery layers or simplified equivalent domains, depending on the level of fidelity adopted. For example, the system may be decomposed into:

  • Electrode regions
  • Separator region
  • Current collectors
  • External casing or thermal boundaries

If the problem is posed in a reduced-order or thermal-only framework, these domains may be simplified while still preserving the main transport and heating mechanisms of interest.

Governing Model

The governing model can be adapted to the scope of the case. Depending on the intended level of detail, it may include:

  • Transient heat conduction with internal heat generation
  • Diffusion equations for concentration fields
  • Potential equations for charge transport
  • Coupled thermal-electrochemical formulations

This section is intended to later include the strong form of the equations, the variational formulation, constitutive assumptions, and the coupling terms used in the FreeFEM implementation.

Numerical Implementation

The model is implemented in FreeFEM using finite element discretization over the selected domains. Depending on the problem, the numerical workflow may involve:

  • Mesh generation for layered or simplified battery geometries
  • Definition of multiple finite element spaces
  • Time integration for transient problems
  • Nonlinear iteration if material properties or source terms depend on state variables
  • Coupling between temperature, potential, and concentration fields

Boundary Conditions

Battery models are highly sensitive to the imposed operating and thermal boundary conditions. Depending on the case, these may include:

  • Current or voltage input during charge/discharge
  • Convective cooling at external surfaces
  • Prescribed temperature boundaries
  • Symmetry conditions in reduced geometries
  • Initial conditions for temperature, concentration, or potential

This part can later be adapted to explain which operating conditions were simulated and why they are relevant for the engineering question of interest.
