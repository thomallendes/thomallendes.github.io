Rotary Kiln Damper Optimization

OpenFOAM documentation of an industrial CFD case involving high-temperature gas-air mixing, species transport, and downstream thermal control in a rotary kiln gas-treatment system.

Overview

This page documents an industrial CFD study developed in OpenFOAM to evaluate the thermal and flow behavior of a rotary kiln damper system. The case focuses on the mixing between high-temperature process gases and ambient air induced by passive suction.

Engineering Context

The system operates under high-temperature conditions in a gas-treatment line associated with a rotary kiln. Controlling downstream temperature and pressure is essential to protect subsequent equipment and maintain stable operating conditions.

Problem Definition

The objective of the study is to determine whether the damper configuration can induce sufficient ambient air entrainment to reduce gas temperature and achieve suitable downstream operating conditions at the measurement zone.

Physical System

The configuration consists of a duct connected to a rotary kiln, where hot gases flow through a rotating damper arrangement that generates a pressure drop and promotes air suction from the surroundings. The resulting gas-air interaction governs the thermal response of the system.

Modeling Assumptions

The flow is modeled as turbulent and compressible, with temperature-dependent properties. A species transport approach is used to describe gas-air mixing, and the case is analyzed under representative steady operating conditions.

Mathematical Model

The model is based on the conservation equations of mass, momentum, energy, and species. These equations describe the coupled evolution of velocity, pressure, temperature, and composition in the gas-treatment domain.

Thermophysical Model

The gas mixture is described using a variable-density thermophysical model based on an ideal gas formulation. This allows density and transport properties to vary consistently with temperature, which is essential under high-temperature conditions.

Geometry and Mesh

The geometry represents the main industrial features of the damper and duct system. The mesh is generated to adequately resolve the mixing region, the damper zone, and areas where large gradients of temperature, velocity, and species concentration are expected.

Boundary Conditions

Boundary conditions are defined to represent the inlet of hot kiln gases, passive air entrainment, outlet pressure behavior, and wall interactions. Temperature and species composition are prescribed to reproduce realistic plant conditions.

Numerical Setup

The problem is solved in OpenFOAM using a finite volume formulation with turbulence modeling, appropriate discretization schemes, and a convergence strategy selected to ensure stable and physically consistent solutions.

Validation and Consistency

The model is assessed through engineering consistency, including mass and thermal balance checks, expected mixing trends, and agreement with physically reasonable downstream pressure and temperature behavior.

Results and Discussion

The results provide insight into the temperature distribution, pressure evolution, gas-air mixing, and flow structure inside the system. Special attention is given to the damper’s effect on downstream thermal conditioning.

Engineering Insights

The analysis highlights the mechanisms that control passive air suction, thermal reduction, and overall system performance. These findings support engineering decisions related to damper design and plant operation.

Model Limitations

The model includes simplifying assumptions such as steady-state operation and turbulence closure, which may limit its ability to capture all transient or highly complex flow features. Even so, it captures the dominant mechanisms relevant to engineering assessment.

Conclusions

This case shows how CFD can be used to analyze and improve the thermal and hydraulic behavior of an industrial gas-treatment system, providing useful criteria for design evaluation and operational decision-making.
